About

Lina Dimitrov is a doctoral candidate in the Department of Epidemiology at Emory University. She received her BA in Neuroscience and Behavior from Barnard College, Columbia University in 2017 and her MPH in Epidemiology and Biostatistics from Boston University in 2020. Her previous work experience between degrees includes teaching English as a foreign language in France, life sciences consulting, and an ORISE Fellowship at the CDC in the National Center on Birth Defects and Developmental Disabilities, where she worked on projects related to children's mental health. Broadly, her interests are in understanding the etiology of neuropsychiatric outcomes, and she is particularly interested in exploring how the environment we live in, including environmental chemicals, the built environment, and psychosocial aspects of the environment, play into the development of these conditions. Currently, her work focuses on exploring how the historic neighborhood exposome relates to development of Alzheimer's disease and other dementias.
